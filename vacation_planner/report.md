# Detailed Report on Savannah, GA

## Introduction
Savannah, Georgia, is a city rich in history and culture. Founded in 1733 by General James Oglethorpe, it is the oldest city in Georgia and one of the most charming in the United States. This report delves into the history, key attractions, and local culinary experiences that make Savannah a must-visit destination.

## History of Savannah

1. **Founding and Early Years**
   - Savannah was established in 1733 by General James Oglethorpe.
   - It is the oldest city in Georgia and the first planned city in the United States, featuring a grid system and 24 squares.
   - Initially conceived as a debtors' prison, the city prohibited lawyers and alcohol.

2. **Civil War Era**
   - During the Civil War, General William Tecumseh Sherman captured Savannah and presented it to President Abraham Lincoln as a Christmas gift in 1864.

3. **First African Baptist Church**
   - The First African Baptist Church, founded in 1773, is the oldest black Baptist church in North America.

4. **Emancipation Proclamation**
   - General David Hunter, based in the Savannah area, made one of the first emancipation proclamations.

## Unique Features and Attractions

1. **24 Historic Squares**
   - Savannah’s grid plan includes 24 historic squares, each with its own unique character and charm.

2. **Spanish Moss**
   - The city's iconic Spanish moss is not actually moss but a flowering plant that drapes the oak trees.

3. **To-Go Cocktails**
   - Savannah allows residents and visitors to take their cocktails to-go, a unique feature among U.S. cities.

4. **Haunted History**
   - Known as one of the most haunted cities in the U.S., Savannah offers numerous ghost tours and haunted attractions.

5. **Forrest Gump Connection**
   - The famous bench scene from the movie "Forrest Gump" was filmed in Savannah.

6. **Antebellum Architecture**
   - The city is renowned for its manicured parks, horse-drawn carriages, and ornate antebellum architecture.

7. **Streetlights**
   - Savannah was the first city in the United States to have streetlights.

## Must-Try Local Foods

1. **Shrimp and Grits**
   - A Southern staple, shrimp and grits combine creamy grits with savory shrimp and a flavorful sauce.

2. **Peach Cobbler**
   - A sweet treat, peach cobbler features fresh peaches topped with a biscuit or cake-like crust.

3. **Fried Green Tomatoes**
   - A tangy and crispy side dish, fried green tomatoes are a Southern classic.

4. **Lowcountry Boil**
   - A communal seafood feast, Lowcountry Boil includes shrimp, crab, sausage, and potatoes seasoned with Cajun spices.

5. **Bagels and Cream Cheese with Garlic Spread**
   - A unique local twist, this breakfast item features bagels topped with cream cheese and a savory garlic spread.

6. **Savannah Moon Pies**
   - A local favorite, these marshmallow-filled snacks come in various flavors and are a sweet treat for any time of day.

## Conclusion
Savannah, GA, offers a rich tapestry of history, culture, and culinary delights. From its founding as a planned city to its unique local foods, Savannah provides an unforgettable experience for every visitor. Whether you're exploring its historic squares, enjoying Southern cuisine, or learning about its haunted past, Savannah is a city that captivates the heart and imagination.
